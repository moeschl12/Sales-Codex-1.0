# ST-0148 — Gap Prospecting: Problem-first in der Kaltakquise

## Statement ID

ST-0148

## Quelle

SRC-0005 — Gap Selling, Keenan, Kapitel 14, PDF S. 78–79

## Originalaussage

> "Gap selling doesn't replace these common and effective prospecting techniques; it adds rocket fuel to them."

> "Prospects will be more willing to meet with you because they will see it's in their best interest to do so."

> "Think about the edge you'll have over the competition if your prospects believe you understand their business and the challenges they face. Imagine how completing a Problem Identification Chart ahead of time could shape the assumptions you'd have about the problems they are dealing with."

> "Prospecting is the first yes. Whenever you ask someone to meet with you, to read your email, or to download your flyer, you're making a sale just like all the other small sales."

## Paraphrase

Gap Selling überträgt seine Problem-zentrierte Logik auch auf die Kaltakquise (Prospecting). Das Ziel ist nicht, das eigene Produkt zu pitchen, sondern dem Prospect zu zeigen, dass man seine Probleme kennt und lösen kann — bevor das erste Gespräch stattfindet. Dafür: PIC vorab ausfüllen (Hypothesen über wahrscheinliche Probleme basierend auf Recherche), dann Outreach mit Problem-Framing statt Feature-Pitch. Prospecting ist der "erste Yes" — dieselbe Mikro-Commitment-Logik wie im Vertriebsprozess gilt hier ab dem ersten Kontakt.
